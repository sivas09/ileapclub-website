# iLEAP Club Member Portal Product Vision

## Purpose

The iLEAP Club Member Portal is the private operating platform for students, parents, facilitators, and administrators.

The portal should make club operations easier, help students take ownership of their learning, give parents visibility into progress, and help iLEAP Club scale across many centres and clubs.

## Parent Perspective

### Goals

- Understand how their child is progressing.
- See upcoming meetings and assigned roles.
- Track attendance, scores, feedback, and band progress.
- Support their child at home.
- Manage more than one child from one parent account.

### Typical Workflow

1. Parent logs in.
2. Parent sees all linked students.
3. Parent selects a child.
4. Parent reviews upcoming meetings.
5. Parent checks role assignments and agenda downloads.
6. Parent reviews attendance, scores, feedback, and band progress.
7. Parent helps the child prepare for the next role or requirement.

### Most Important Screens

- Parent dashboard
- Student profile summary
- Upcoming meetings
- Meeting agenda download
- Attendance history
- Scores and feedback
- Band progress
- Requirements for next band level

### Most Important Reports

- Student progress summary
- Attendance summary
- Score history
- Role participation history
- Band level progress report
- Missing requirements report

### Pain Points Solved

- Parents no longer need to ask repeatedly what happened in class.
- Parents can see whether their child is participating.
- Parents can understand what the child needs to improve.
- Parents can manage multiple children in one place.
- Progress becomes visible instead of informal.

## Student Perspective

### Goals

- Know what meetings are coming up.
- Claim open roles independently.
- Prepare for assigned roles.
- Download the agenda.
- See scores, feedback, and band progress.
- Understand what is needed to reach the next level.

### Typical Workflow

1. Student logs in.
2. Student sees upcoming meetings.
3. Student opens a meeting.
4. Student claims an available role.
5. Student downloads the agenda.
6. Student prepares for the meeting.
7. After the meeting, student reviews score and feedback.
8. Student checks progress toward the next band level.

### Most Important Screens

- Student dashboard
- Upcoming meetings
- Meeting details
- Open roles
- My claimed roles
- Agenda download
- Scores and feedback
- Band progress
- Next requirements

### Most Important Reports

- My scores
- My attendance
- My role history
- My band progress
- What I need next

### Pain Points Solved

- Students do not need to wait for a facilitator to assign every role.
- Students can take ownership of participation.
- Students know what to prepare before each meeting.
- Students can see their improvement over time.
- Students understand how to move to the next band level.

## Facilitator Perspective

### Goals

- Run organized meetings.
- Publish meetings and role opportunities.
- Let students self-claim roles.
- Override role assignments when needed.
- Generate meeting agendas.
- Mark attendance.
- Enter scores and feedback.
- Track student progress.

### Typical Workflow

1. Facilitator logs in.
2. Facilitator selects assigned club.
3. Facilitator creates or reviews an upcoming meeting.
4. Facilitator selects an agenda template.
5. Facilitator publishes the meeting.
6. Students claim roles.
7. Facilitator reviews assignments and overrides if needed.
8. Facilitator downloads the agenda.
9. After the meeting, facilitator marks attendance.
10. Facilitator enters scores and feedback.
11. Facilitator reviews band progress.

### Most Important Screens

- Facilitator dashboard
- Assigned clubs
- Meeting list
- Meeting editor
- Role assignment board
- Agenda preview/download
- Attendance screen
- Score entry screen
- Student progress view

### Most Important Reports

- Meeting readiness report
- Unfilled roles report
- Attendance report
- Score report
- Student progress report
- Band advancement readiness report

### Pain Points Solved

- Facilitators do not need to manually assign every role.
- Agendas can be generated with names already filled in.
- Attendance and scores are stored consistently.
- Facilitators can quickly see who is participating.
- Role overrides are tracked and transparent.
- Student progress is easier to review.

## Admin Perspective

### Goals

- Manage many centres and clubs.
- Manage students, parents, facilitators, and admins.
- Assign members to clubs.
- Assign facilitators to clubs.
- Standardize roles, agenda templates, scores, and band levels.
- Support scalable operations.
- Prepare the platform for future competitions and resources.

### Typical Workflow

1. Admin logs in.
2. Admin creates or updates centres.
3. Admin creates clubs under centres.
4. Admin adds users.
5. Admin links parents and students.
6. Admin assigns students and facilitators to clubs.
7. Admin manages role definitions.
8. Admin manages agenda templates.
9. Admin reviews attendance, scores, and band progress.
10. Admin handles exceptions and overrides.

### Most Important Screens

- Admin dashboard
- User management
- Parent-student relationship management
- Centre management
- Club management
- Membership management
- Role management
- Agenda template management
- Band level management
- Reporting dashboard

### Most Important Reports

- Centre performance report
- Club enrollment report
- Attendance report
- Student progress report
- Facilitator activity report
- Band level distribution report
- Role participation report
- Competition readiness report

### Pain Points Solved

- Operations can scale beyond one club.
- Admins can manage centres and clubs separately.
- Parent-student relationships are organized.
- Facilitator assignments are clear.
- Band progress is standardized.
- Reports help leadership understand program health.

## User Stories

### Parent User Stories

As a Parent, I want to see all my children in one dashboard, so that I can manage their iLEAP Club progress easily.

As a Parent, I want to see my child's upcoming meetings, so that I know when they need to prepare.

As a Parent, I want to see my child's claimed role, so that I can help them practice at home.

As a Parent, I want to download the meeting agenda, so that I understand what will happen in the meeting.

As a Parent, I want to see my child's attendance, so that I know whether they are participating consistently.

As a Parent, I want to see my child's scores and feedback, so that I can help them improve.

As a Parent, I want to see my child's band progress, so that I know what level they are working toward.

As a Parent, I want to see missing band requirements, so that I know what my child needs next.

### Student User Stories

As a Student, I want to see upcoming meetings, so that I know what is coming next.

As a Student, I want to claim an open role, so that I can participate in the next meeting.

As a Student, I want to see which roles are still open, so that I can choose how to contribute.

As a Student, I want to download the agenda, so that I can prepare before the meeting.

As a Student, I want to see my scores, so that I understand how I performed.

As a Student, I want to read facilitator feedback, so that I know what to improve.

As a Student, I want to see my band level, so that I know my progress.

As a Student, I want to see requirements for the next band, so that I know what to work on.

### Facilitator User Stories

As a Facilitator, I want to create a meeting, so that my club has a clear upcoming schedule.

As a Facilitator, I want to select an agenda template, so that the meeting follows the right format.

As a Facilitator, I want students to claim open roles, so that I do not assign every role manually.

As a Facilitator, I want to override role assignments, so that I can keep the meeting balanced and fair.

As a Facilitator, I want to generate an agenda, so that meetings are organized.

As a Facilitator, I want to mark attendance, so that participation is tracked.

As a Facilitator, I want to enter scores and feedback, so that students know how they performed.

As a Facilitator, I want to see student progress, so that I can support each student better.

### Admin User Stories

As an Admin, I want to manage centres, so that iLEAP Club can operate in multiple locations.

As an Admin, I want to manage clubs, so that each centre can have multiple groups.

As an Admin, I want to manage users, so that students, parents, facilitators, and admins have correct access.

As an Admin, I want to link parents to students, so that parents can view the right children.

As an Admin, I want to assign students to clubs, so that memberships are accurate.

As an Admin, I want to assign facilitators to clubs, so that responsibilities are clear.

As an Admin, I want to manage agenda templates, so that Junior, Senior, Debate, Competition, and Town Hall meetings are standardized.

As an Admin, I want to manage band requirements, so that progress is consistent across clubs.

As an Admin, I want to view reports, so that I can understand program growth and quality.
